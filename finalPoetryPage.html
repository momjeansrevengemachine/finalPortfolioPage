to be loved by a poet

is the greatest, softest, most crushing experience known to man. They will destroy the words you loved, bring meaning to the prepositions which once meant nothing. You see bodies with no capitalization and remember me. I turned off auto-caps on my phone years ago and never looked back. (The innate urge for drama.) If one day I turned it back on I think you would be nervous. Of who I’ve become.

Do you sweat when you think about me? Do you remember when you broke me into pieces to mold me back into the version you wanted? Now the perforations on my body allow me to rebuild faster than ever. You tore me apart seam from seam but I found out what I was made of finally and now I sew myself back together with every word I write.

To be loved by a poet is to fear your own substance. They will see all of it. You can’t hide your intentions from a writer who looks right through your screen and reads the script notes you thought you could hide. You can direct the show and tell yourself it’s kept you safe but didn’t you know that TV is never real? I will not play your part any more.

Instead, I immortalize you with the keys under my hand. I wish you still played. I asked you to try for me, to grow with me, but still the piano sits, broken, untuned, waiting for my voice to write a melody to keep us going.

i cut my bangs

but they're too short

i cut my eyes so i can't see

the curls on the floor won't cry for me

the glasses on the sink can't save me

can you see me from the ground

will you cut my hair

until you rid yourself of me

i pack the boxes who can't hear me

the tape stuck to itself won't fix me

can the dust on the floor

bring me back down?

I'm too afraid to leave.

you play my voice and i almost hear it

but the ghost in the mirror doesn't know me

who's haunting who?

an eye for an eye but you blinded me long ago

have you seen the inside?

have you pulled back the layers with your hands

washed yourself of a nothingness that creates a whole world

have you seen through the eyes of another or is all you can see the bleeding scars on your surface

i couldn’t see through the tears of red so i cut my hair and i fucked it up

and it looks fine but my week is ruined until it grows back out

and my months are ruined until i outgrow you

but i’ve cut off all the ends you’ve touched

it’s softer now without you

i’m fuller now without you thinning my hair

without you starving my heart and draining my eyes

would you stitch them back together

or is the scar tissue too thick

would you tie my hair back or are the ends too broken

maybe i should start cutting my hair with a mirror

living the midwest gothic

driving down a freeway that looks an awful lot like a farmland hellscape, billboards tell you hell is real

and another mile down is the “gentleman’s club” where you find the entrance, hidden in loose glitter and shoes that defy gravity

a cheap girl for a good night but another mile down will reminder you that abortion is murder

control bodies for a dollar and the love of the game

she only matters after your paid minutes

if it’s another chance to remind her she’s not in control

another mile down, budget fireworks

all celebration must induce fear

one more mile. guns.

no requirements, walk in and out with a desire to hurt. no questions asked.

one more mile. god is real.

is this an attempt to save us from these last few miles? or a reminder that we’ll never meet?

why are there so many fucking billboards on country roads anyways. we all know there’s an escape, but nobody takes the next exit
